# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Clementine should be a constructive, rigorous, and welcoming project. Contributors, maintainers, and community members are expected to treat one another with respect regardless of experience, identity, background, or point of view.

## Expected behavior

- Be specific, kind, and focused on the work.
- Assume good intent while still addressing concrete impact.
- Welcome questions and explain unfamiliar project conventions.
- Give actionable review feedback and accept it professionally.
- Respect privacy, consent, authorship, and confidentiality.
- Disclose conflicts of interest that could affect a technical decision.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, stalking, or sustained personal attacks;
- sexualized language or unwanted attention;
- publishing another person's private information or credentials;
- deliberately introducing malicious code, deceptive behavior, or unsafe instructions;
- using project spaces for spam, intimidation, or unrelated promotion;
- retaliating against someone who raises a good-faith concern.

Technical disagreement is welcome. Contempt, identity-based attacks, and conduct that makes participation unsafe are not.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, project-managed community spaces, and public interactions where someone is representing Clementine.

## Reporting and enforcement

For a private project-level conduct report, use the repository's [private reporting form](https://github.com/Natebreynolds/clemmy/security/advisories/new) and begin the title with `[Conduct]`. Do not place personal details in a public issue. GitHub's platform reporting and blocking tools are also available for conduct that violates GitHub's policies.

Maintainers will review reports in context, protect private information where practical, and may remove content, issue a warning, limit participation, or permanently exclude a participant. Knowingly false or retaliatory reports may themselves violate this policy.

Anyone asked to stop unacceptable behavior is expected to comply immediately.
